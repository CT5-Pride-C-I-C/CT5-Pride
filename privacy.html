Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2024

Who We Are

CT5 Pride is a Community Interest Company based in Whitstable, Kent, UK. We are committed to protecting your privacy and being transparent about how we use your information.

What Data We Collect and Why

  • Name – to identify you for applications and communication
  • Email address – to contact you about your application or volunteering
  • CV files (uploaded via volunteer applications) – to assess your suitability for volunteer roles
  • Application details – information you provide about your experience, interests, and role preferences
  • Role interest areas – to match you with suitable opportunities

Third-Party Services We Use

We use trusted third-party services to help us run CT5 Pride securely and efficiently. Your data may be processed by:

  • Supabase – for secure storage of application and CV data
  • Render – for backend infrastructure (Node server)
  • Netlify – for static site hosting
  • GitHub – for version control and admin content commits (no personal data)
  • Eventbrite – for ticketing and RSVP functionality
  • Zoho CRM (planned) – for community updates and email contact management
  • Jotform (planned) – for community stallholder registration forms
  • Mailchimp (planned) – for email newsletters

Personal data is only shared with these providers when necessary and always under secure conditions.

How We Keep Your Data Safe

We take your privacy seriously. We protect your information using secure technology and safe practices:

  • We use encrypted (HTTPS) connections so your information is protected in transit.
  • Your CVs and personal data are stored in a secure, private Supabase database and file storage.
  • Only authorised members of CT5 Pride’s admin team can access your data.
  • Admin access is protected using Supabase login and identity checks.
  • We never publish your personal data or sell it to other companies.
  • We regularly update our systems and delete your data when it’s no longer needed.

How Long We Keep Your Data

  • CVs and applications from unsuccessful candidates are deleted within 6 months.
  • Anonymised statistical data (e.g., total applications per role) may be kept for longer for reporting and improvement purposes.

Your Rights (UK GDPR)

  • You can ask to see the data we hold about you at any time.
  • You can request deletion or correction of your data.
  • You can withdraw your consent for us to use your data at any time.
  • You can complain to the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) if you are unhappy with how we handle your data.
